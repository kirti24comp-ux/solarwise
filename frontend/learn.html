Solar Energy Basics

How Solar Works

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity through photovoltaic cells. When sunlight hits the panels, it creates an electric field that generates DC electricity, which an inverter converts to AC power for your home.

Key Components

  • Solar Panels: Capture sunlight
  • Inverter: Converts DC to AC power
  • Racking: Mounts panels to roof
  • Monitoring: Tracks system performance
  • Battery (optional): Stores excess energy

System Types

  • Grid-Tied: Connected to utility, no battery
  • Hybrid: Grid-connected with battery backup
  • Off-Grid: Complete independence with batteries

Solar Potential Factors

✅ What Helps

  • South-facing roof (north-facing in southern hemisphere)
  • Minimal shade from trees/buildings
  • Roof age under 10 years
  • High electricity rates

❌ What Hinders

  • Heavy shade throughout day
  • North-facing roof (northern hemisphere)
  • Roof needing replacement soon
  • HOA restrictions

Quick Solar Math

1 kW of solar panels typically produces:

  • 3-5 kWh per day (varies by location)
  • 1,000-1,500 kWh per year
  • Requires about 100 sq ft of roof space

Average US home uses ~900 kWh per month and needs 6-8 kW system
